THE CONSTITUTION



  • The Great Dykshoorn administers the bestowment of power upon all positions
  • The Great shall be bestowed the power to amend any and all changes to the constitution and public legislation proposed by the people regarding scientific needs
  • The Great Dykshoorn shall be vested with the executive power to veto all decisions regarding the advancement of society if deemed unaligned with scientific needs.


  • Citizens must complete a minimum of one physics course in every year of education, with questions that must be completed on a daily basis
  • No citizen may question the difficulty of the problems as the wisdom of the Great Dykshoorn guarantees that every single physics problem will be easy, trivial, or breezy.
  • Every citizen is expected to solve every problem by inspection.
  • Every citizen is required to acknowledge the superiority of the nation of Japan.


  • All citizens herein shall be bestowed the right attend physics courses - defined as:
  • Biology: squishy physics
  • Chemistry: impure physics
  • Math: physics without units
  • Psychology: people physics
  • Computer Science: virtual physics
  • Geology: slow physics


    • In the face of law, all citizens may call upon physics teachers - and ONLY physics teachers - as bearers of rightful judgement.
